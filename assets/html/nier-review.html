Nier: Automata

Score: [9.8/10] Genre: [Action JRPG] Platform: [PC, Playstation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch] Release: [March 17, 2017] [PC]

Overview

Nier:Automata is a story-driven action JRPG developed by PlatinumGames that was a whole new experience for me. I rarely play single-player games but this truly left its mark. When anyone asks me my favority game, I say Nier: Automata with no hesitation.

Gameplay

The gameplay is centered around exploring an open world and fighting enemies. The world that was built was truly atonishing and I will go over more in Story section. The combat is a mix of action-based hack and slash with various weapons and abilities. As the player progresses, they level up and gain access to character customization through Chips. These Chips can alter how you play and what abilities you have access to. At some points, they game will change genres such as shoot 'em ups or text adventure segments. Nier: Automata has 26 different endings; five main letter endings (A-E) and 21 additional endings (F-Z).

Story

Before I begin, I want to say that the best way to experience the story is to play the game itself, I will not spoil anything regarding the store in this review. Nier is told across multiple playthroughs following the perspectives of the characters 2B and 9S primarily. They are androids that deal with attacks against machines and defend the Resistance. These machines begin to display human characteristics and replicating their societies. I can't devulge more into the story but what you learn about the world and the stories the characters go through, especially in the True Ending E, leaves such a lasting impact on the player. I wil reiterate again, please play this game and experience it yourself.

Visuals and Audio

I have to say, the music in this game, along with the atmosphere that the devlopers created, is truly one of a kind. I have included the song you experience at the end of the game because it is just that good. There are many other great soundtracks that I implore you listen to as well. It is even more immersive when you play the game and listen to the music they have for each region, they made it feel like you are truly experiencing the world and story. An fun fact about the both the composer, Keiichi Okaba, and the artist, Emi Evans, when making the songs, they went through multiple demos to come up with futuristic language that sounded like all the languages combined after the passage of time. When you listen, even if it techincally is just gibberish, it sounds like a real language.

Nier: Automata OST - Weight of the World

Performance

I played this game on my Xbox One back in the day and it ran relatively well, so I would say for all consoles, especially the new gen, you shouldn't have to worry about any performance issues. For PC, I have attached the specs from Steam's store page for the game. As far as I can remember, I don't think I encountered any major bugs or issues when playing, if there was anything, they were relatively minor and shouldn't be a problem for most players.

Final Verdict

Overall, Nier: Automata is a masterpiece that everyone should play at least once. I remember my time during a summer where I decided to give it a try and spent a majority of my time just playing it. It was baffling to me that I never even debated about trying this game earlier. When I finished the game, it left a hole in me that was hard to replace and felt so empty for a week or two, it was that impactful to me. In the end, I implore you to give this game a try and experience all that its worth. Truly a 9.8/10.
